# Warranty & RMA Claims Desk — Resolution Execution Completion (Round 4)

> **Parent specs:** [`2026-07-03-warranty-rma-claims-desk.md`](./2026-07-03-warranty-rma-claims-desk.md) (foundation), [`2026-07-05-warranty-rma-claims-desk-completion.md`](./2026-07-05-warranty-rma-claims-desk-completion.md) (world-class completion), [`2026-07-16-warranty-rma-competitive-refinements.md`](./2026-07-16-warranty-rma-competitive-refinements.md) (round 3: 19-product benchmark + first execution bridge). This round takes the next two items off round 3's triaged roadmap — the **replacement-order bridge** and the **credit-memo bridge** — completing the resolution-execution story the benchmark identified as the #1 competitive gap, and closes out the round-3 implementation-council survivors.

## TLDR

**Key Points:**
- **Round 3 shipped one third of resolution execution.** An approved claim can now mint its sales return with one click, but `replace`-disposition lines still end with staff hand-building a replacement order in sales, and `credit`/`refund` dispositions still end with staff hand-writing a credit memo. Every ERP-grade competitor executes all three documents from the RMA (D365 replace dispositions attach the replacement sales order; NetSuite sets replacement items to **zero amount** on the RMA-generated sales order and mints the credit memo from the received RMA; Odoo return-for-exchange spawns the outbound transfer; Saleor `replace: true` creates the draft replacement order).
- **Phase 1 — `warranty_claims.claim.create_replacement_order`:** builds a replacement sales order from `replace`-disposition lines via `commandBus` dispatch of `sales.orders.create` (verified contract, citations below), zero-priced by default (NetSuite convention), copying customer/addresses/currency from the original order, stamping `replacementOrderId` under a pessimistic lock with a single unambiguous compensation boundary + version-token undo — the proven round-3 bridge shape.
- **Phase 2 — `warranty_claims.claim.create_credit_memo`:** mints the credit memo for `credit`/`refund`-disposition lines via `sales.credit_memos.create`, **receipt-proven per line** (line status received/inspected/resolved AND credited quantity capped by `qtyReceived` — NetSuite gates credit after the item receipt), amounts mirroring the module's own approved-rollup semantics (`max(0, credit − restockingFee + coreCredit)`, `lib/stateMachine.ts:110`) with a fully specified percentage-aware tax and half-up rounding contract. One additive migration: `warranty_claims.credit_memo_id`.
- **Phase 3 — hygiene:** the eight round-3 council-survivor edits plus one round-4 cross-bridge alignment (nine edits total, see Phase 3).
- Staff-explicit action everywhere — no automation mints documents; the module's adjudication philosophy (signals inform, humans decide) extends to execution (documents are one click, never zero clicks).

**Scope boundary:** additive, single-module plus sanctioned `commandBus` dispatch of `sales.orders.{create,delete}` and `sales.credit_memos.{create,delete}`, scoped cross-module *reads*, and **one narrowly-scoped, test-anticipated sales fix**: `sales.credit_memos.create` validates `orderId` but never persists the order link — `TC-SALES-031.spec.ts:79-81` pins the null and states it "will fail — by design — once the link is persisted, prompting a flip to `expect(created.order_id).toBe(orderId)`". Phase 2 persists the link and flips that assertion, exactly as the sales test invites. No new claim statuses, no new ACL features, no event-id changes, no FROZEN-surface changes. One additive migration (one nullable uuid column).

**Scope cohesion note (spec-writing gate):** the two bridges are the same capability (resolution execution) applied to the two remaining document types, built on the same round-3 pattern; the hygiene phase is bounded cleanup of that same pattern's first instance. Each phase is independently shippable and droppable, per this module's established multi-phase convention.

## Problem Statement

Round 3's benchmark table row **"Resolution execution (credit/replacement/return docs)"** scored ✅ for returns SaaS, warranty platforms, and ERPs, and ❌ "links only" for this module. Round 3 upgraded returns to ✅; replacement orders and credit memos remain ❌:

1. **`replace` dispositions produce no order.** Staff select the `replace` disposition — then leave the module, hand-create a sales order, hand-zero its prices, and hand-paste its id into the `replacementOrderId` picker. D365 creates the replacement order from the disposition (manually before receipt for immediate shipment, or automatically after arrival/inspection/receipt); NetSuite's replacement claim "sets the amount of the warranty item to zero" on the SO it creates from the RMA.
2. **`credit`/`refund` dispositions produce no credit memo.** The claim knows the approved and received quantities, the order lines, what the customer actually paid per unit (discount-aware line totals), the per-line `creditAmount` overrides, `restockingFee` deductions and `coreCreditAmount` additions — and throws all of it away at the finish line. NetSuite mints the credit memo from the received RMA in one click.
3. **The claim entity cannot even record a credit memo.** `salesReturnId` and `replacementOrderId` exist as link fields; there is no `creditMemoId` — the money document is untracked even when staff create it manually.
4. **Round 3 left documented survivors** — small correctness/completeness debts in the first bridge (openApi under-documentation, an undocumented input alias, a missing integration assertion, dead selected columns, incomplete flash copy, one helper-type omission, one missing unit test).

## Market Reference

Round 3's 19-product benchmark (July 2026) remains the source of record; this round re-verified the two design-critical conventions:

- **Replacement pricing:** NetSuite documents that for RMA-generated replacement sales orders "the amount of the warranty item is set to zero", with *Ship Replacement in Advance* covering pre-receipt dispatch ([Creating a Sales Order for a Replacement Claim](https://docs.oracle.com/en/cloud/saas/netsuite/ns-online-help/bridgehead_3938326430.html), [Managing Refund, Repair, and Replacement Claims](https://docs.oracle.com/en/cloud/saas/netsuite/ns-online-help/section_4391753773.html)). D365's disposition actions (`Replace and credit`, `Replace and scrap`) attach a second sales order to the RMA, created manually before receipt for immediate shipment or automatically after inspection ([Sales returns](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/dynamics365/supply-chain/sales-marketing/sales-returns), [Set up disposition codes](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/dynamics365/supply-chain/service-management/set-up-disposition-codes)).
- **Credit-memo gating:** NetSuite exposes the credit-memo button on the RMA **after the item receipt**; the memo is then applied to balance or refunded. Both platforms treat memo creation as an explicit staff action on a received return — never automatic.

## Proposed Solution — four phases

### Shared bridge mechanics (both phases)

- **Single post-create failure boundary (reconciles round-4 jury F-05; token-read semantics per round-3 F-01):** after the nested `sales.*.create` dispatch succeeds, EVERY **thrown** failure on the way to a successfully flushed claim stamp — stamp-transaction failure or lost race — triggers the compensating `sales.*.delete` dispatch (scrubbed context; full scoped input for memos). A failure of the nested `sales.*.create` dispatch itself needs no compensation — nothing was created; known peer rejections translate to the bridge's named 400s and anything else propagates as-is (r4 F-18). The undo-token read cannot throw: `querySalesReferenceRow`'s catch swallows every error into `undefined` (`claims.ts:798-801`), so a null/undefined result for the just-created row degrades the stored token to `null` — the stamp proceeds normally and only the undo weakens (it will abort 409 on the missing token; established round-3 semantics, no compensation warranted because no state is corrupt). When compensation succeeds: a lost race surfaces as 400 `*AlreadyLinked`, any other failure rethrows the original error. When compensation itself fails: the command ALWAYS surfaces a 500-class orphan error (never the underlying 400), logs the orphaned document id, and best-effort appends an orphan timeline entry on a fresh EM; the peer document itself carries the claim number (order `metadata.warrantyClaimNumber` / memo `reason`), so the orphan is discoverable from the sales side even if the timeline write also fails. No silent success in any branch.
- **Undo (version-token compare-then-delete, round-3 F-28 discipline; three branches):** the undo payload stores the created document's `updated_at` read via scoped lookup immediately after creation. Undo re-reads it: (a) exact match → dispatch the peer delete (scrubbed, full scoped input for memos) and restore the claim snapshot inside `withAtomicFlush({ transaction: true })`; (b) mismatch or missing token while the row exists → 409 abort (`*ChangedUndoAborted`), claim stamp NOT cleared; (c) **peer row definitively absent** — the scoped lookup returns **`null`** (query succeeded, no live row: deleted or soft-deleted in sales since) → skip the delete dispatch, restore the claim snapshot, info-log — the document is already gone and undo must not be blocked forever (round-2 jury F-14). **A degraded re-read (`undefined` — missing table or a masked infra error, the helper's other return) is NOT absence: it aborts like branch (b)** — otherwise a transient DB blip during undo would clear the claim link while the sales document still exists, manufacturing exactly the orphan this design fights (spec-council r4 blocker F-01/F-15). A retried undo after any partial failure converges: re-reads re-classify the current state, and branch (c)'s null handling makes a second delete attempt impossible (r4 F-08). Neither peer delete enforces an optimistic lock (verified above), so the explicit compare is the only stale-guard. **Accepted residuals (round-3 jury F-25, same class as round-3's accepted residual):** the compare→delete window is a benign TOCTOU (an edit landing inside it is deleted with the document — the loud path already warned via the compare on any earlier edit); and if the peer delete succeeds but the snapshot restore then fails, the claim keeps a stamp pointing at a deleted document — the undo surfaces a loud error carrying both ids and staff clear the link manually via the edit page. The compare also assumes peer mutations bump the header row's `updated_at` — true for the sales command surface (every order/memo command touches the header) but not for hypothetical raw SQL edits; accepted with the same residual status (r4 F-20).
- **Null-omission rule (round-2 jury F-03/F-04):** dispatch inputs are built key-by-key; any source value that is null/undefined is OMITTED (sales create schemas reject `null` on every optional field). Applies to customer/channel/address-id fields, line `name`, and every other passthrough.
- **Order-line ownership check (round-2 jury F-15):** each resolved source order line must satisfy `order_id === claim.orderId`; a mismatched line (claim re-linked to a different order after lines were added) is skipped into `skippedLineIds` — neither `sales.orders.create` nor `sales.credit_memos.create` provides a sales-side backstop for line↔order consistency (unlike `sales.returns.create`).
- **Eligibility is re-checked after per-line degradation (jury F-11):** lines skipped because their sales rows are missing/unusable land in `skippedLineIds`; if the eligible set becomes empty at ANY point before dispatch, the command answers 400 `*NoEligibleLines`.
- **Scoped lookups:** extend `SalesReferenceDb` (`claims.ts:740-763`) with the source-order projection columns (Phase 1) and `sales_credit_memos: { id, updated_at }` (Phase 2 undo token + manual-link validation). All lookups tenant/org-scoped with `to_regclass`-equivalent `42P01` degradation, per the round-3 pattern.
- **Route shape:** both new routes are POST-only, `.strict()` request schemas declaring exactly `claimId` (+ documented optionals) — **no `id` alias on new routes**; mutation guards via `runRouteMutationGuards` (resourceKind `WARRANTY_CLAIM_RESOURCE_KIND`, operation `custom`); optimistic-lock header honored for the claim; openApi documents **200, 400, 401, 403, 409**.

### Phase 1 — Replacement-order bridge

**Command** `warranty_claims.claim.create_replacement_order` (new, in `commands/claims.ts`, `isUndoable: true`, mirroring `create_sales_return` at `claims.ts:2115`):

- **Input** (command) `{ organizationId, tenantId, id: uuid, pricing?: 'zero' | 'original' (default 'zero'), updatedAt?: token }` `.strict()`.
- **Guards:** scoped claim + `enforceWarrantyClaimOptimisticLock`; `orderId` present (400 `replacementOrderRequiresOrder`); status ∈ `REPLACEMENT_ELIGIBLE_CLAIM_STATUSES = approved | awaiting_return | received | inspecting | resolved` (approved/awaiting_return = NetSuite-style advance replacement; received/inspecting = D365-style post-inspection; `resolved` included so a claim resolved before the document was minted is not locked out — round-3 jury F-02. **Status asymmetry rationale:** the credit set is strictly post-receipt because money follows the goods; the replacement set additionally allows pre-receipt states because advance replacement is the NetSuite-documented flow — round-3 jury F-23); `replacementOrderId` empty (400 `replacementAlreadyLinked`); sales present via `resolveOptionalEntityId('sales', 'sales_order')` (400 `replacementSalesUnavailable`).
- **Disposition workflow note (jury F-23):** `disposition` is settable from intake onward — `claim_line.create` accepts it (`commands/claim-lines.ts:321`) and `claim_line.update` sets it at any line status (`:355`), gated only by claim-type compatibility (`:693`) and grade compatibility once graded (`:695`). Staff therefore mark `replace` at adjudication for advance replacement; receiving/grading refines dispositions later. The path is reachable pre-receipt by design.
- **Line eligibility:** non-deleted ∧ `disposition === 'replace'` ∧ `lineStatus ∈ approved | received | inspected | resolved` ∧ `orderLineId` present ∧ effective qty (`qtyApproved ?? qtyClaimed`) > 0 ∧ whole units (`% CLAIMED_QUANTITY_SCALE === 0n` — replacements ship as discrete goods) ∧ the source order line resolves and carries an identity (`product_id` OR `product_variant_id` OR non-empty `name` — an identity-less line cannot form a meaningful order line and is skipped, jury F-26). Everything else → `skippedLineIds`; empty set (before or after degradation) → 400 `replacementNoEligibleLines`. A claim whose lines are all `credit` produces no replacement order — the disposition is the semantic trigger (D365 convention).
- **Source-order read:** extend the `sales_orders` projection with `currency_code`, `customer_entity_id`, `customer_contact_id`, `billing_address_id`, `shipping_address_id`, `channel_id`, **and `updated_at`** (the undo-token read selects it — round-3 jury F-17/F-19; columns verified on `SalesOrder`, `sales/data/entities.ts`; null fields are omitted from the dispatch input per the null-omission rule). **The encrypted snapshot columns are deliberately NOT read (impl-council r2 F-04):** `customer_snapshot`/`billing_address_snapshot`/`shipping_address_snapshot` are in the sales encryption map (`sales/encryption.ts:7-9`) and the module's scoped kysely reads bypass decryption — copying them would silently drop (or leak ciphertext of) customer data on encryption-enabled tenants. The bridge passes the plain `billingAddressId`/`shippingAddressId` uuid columns instead and `sales.orders.create` rebuilds fresh snapshots through its own decryption-aware `resolveAddressSnapshot` (`documents.ts:720-753`) — the same path the admin UI uses. Orders that carry only snapshots (no address ids) yield a replacement order without addresses; the operator completes it in sales (honest degradation, flash-visible). Missing order row → 400 `replacementOrderRequiresOrder`. Per-line reads extend `sales_order_lines` with `product_id`, `product_variant_id`, `name`, `kind`, `currency_code`, `unit_price_net`, `unit_price_gross`, `tax_rate` (`kind` is copied onto the replacement line, so it must be selected — r4 F-13; prices/rate non-null with `'0'` defaults — `sales/data/entities.ts` `SalesOrderLine`, jury F-21). Replacement lines keep each source line's own `currency_code` verbatim — the bridge never sums across replacement lines (sales recomputes totals per line), so no cross-line currency guard is needed there, unlike the memo (r4 F-26).
- **Nested dispatch:** `sales.orders.create` (scrubbed ctx) with `{ organizationId, tenantId, currencyCode: order.currency_code, metadata: { warrantyClaimId, warrantyClaimNumber }, lines }` plus — **only when non-null on the source row** (null-omission rule) — `customerEntityId`, `customerContactId`, `billingAddressId`, `shippingAddressId`, `channelId` (snapshots rebuilt by sales; see the encryption rationale above). Each line: `{ productId?, productVariantId?, name?, kind: source line kind (copied — a replace-disposition service line replicates as a service line, round-2 jury F-12), currencyCode: line.currency_code, quantity: effectiveQty }` with null identity fields omitted per the rule; pricing fields by mode: `'zero'` → `unitPriceNet: '0', unitPriceGross: '0'` (no `taxRate` — a zero-priced line carries no tax); `'original'` → `unitPriceNet`, `unitPriceGross`, `taxRate` copied verbatim from the order line (jury F-18). `orderNumber` omitted (auto-generated); no `statusEntryId` (sales' dictionary-driven flow applies); no totals passed (recomputed regardless).
- **Advance-replacement stamp:** when claim status at execution is `approved | awaiting_return`, the stamp transaction also sets `advanceReplacement = true` (existing column). `advanceShippedAt` untouched — shipping belongs to sales.
- **Stamp sequence:** (1) validate + lock the claim outside any transaction; (2) dispatch the nested create; (3) read the order's `updated_at` (undo token); (4) short `PESSIMISTIC_WRITE` transaction: reload claim, re-check `replacementOrderId IS NULL`, stamp `replacementOrderId` (+ `advanceReplacement` when derived), append `system` timeline event (`warranty_claims.timeline.replacementOrderCreated`, internal, payload `{ replacementOrderId, pricing }`), flush; (5) any step-3/4 failure → shared failure boundary above. Then the standard claim CRUD-updated side effect (cache/index refresh).
- **Result:** `{ claimId, replacementOrderId, replacementOrderUpdatedAt, skippedLineIds, pricing }`.

**API** `POST /api/warranty_claims/replacement-order` — body `{ claimId: uuid, pricing?: 'zero' | 'original', updatedAt?: datetime | null }` (`.strict()`) → **200** `{ replacementOrderId, skippedLineIds }`; 400 `replacementOrderRequiresOrder | replacementInvalidStatus | replacementNoEligibleLines | replacementAlreadyLinked | replacementSalesUnavailable`; 401/403; 409 (claim lock). Features `warranty_claims.claim.manage` **+ `sales.orders.manage`** (the exact feature sales' own order-create route requires). openApi: full response set.
- **UI:** "Create replacement order" outline button in the detail page's fulfillment action block (beside "Create sales return", `[id]/page.tsx:1771`), visible when `featureAccess.claimManage ∧ featureAccess.salesOrdersManage ∧ claim.orderId ∧ !claim.replacementOrderId ∧ status eligible`; `useGuardedMutation` + `withScopedApiRequestHeaders(buildOptimisticLockHeader(claim.updatedAt), …)`; success flash + skipped-lines info flash (copy names all skip reasons: no linked order line, ineligible status/disposition, zero/fractional quantity, unresolvable order line) + `loadData()` reload; 409 via `surfaceRecordConflict`. The UI always sends the default pricing (`zero`); `original` is an API-level affordance for paid-exchange flows until a picker earns its place (roadmap). Note `pricing: 'original'` copies the source lines' **catalog unit prices, pre-discount** — the replacement order is a fresh document, not a financial mirror; the memo bridge is the money-accurate artifact (the openApi description spells out the pre-discount semantics — round-3 jury F-22).

### Phase 2 — Credit-memo bridge

**Sales prerequisite (test-anticipated fix, round-2 jury F-01; scoped to the ORDER link only, round-3 jury F-03/F-24):** `createCreditMemoCommand` never assigns the order relation it validates — the `em.create` block passes an `orderId` key but the entity property is the `ManyToOne` relation `order` (`entities.ts:1554`), so `sales_credit_memos.order_id` persists null; `TC-SALES-031.spec.ts:79-81` pins this and explicitly anticipates the flip. Fix: assign the validated order reference in the `em.create` block (`documents.ts:8998` region), flip TC-SALES-031's assertion to `expect(created.order_id).toBe(orderId)`, add a sales credit-memo unit case for the link, and record the behavior change in `UPGRADE_NOTES.md` (round-3 jury F-15). The invoice link is explicitly OUT of scope — whether it shares the gap is a separate sales concern (roadmap note), keeping this fix single-purpose and fully covered. Without it, the bridge's memo would not be traceable from sales and TC-WC-034's `order_id` assertion cannot pass.

**Migration + entity:** additive `credit_memo_id uuid null` on `warranty_claims` (`Migration<ts>_warranty_claims.ts` + `.snapshot-open-mercato.json` in the same change; `down()` drops it). Entity field `creditMemoId` beside `replacementOrderId`; joins `CLAIM_FULFILLMENT_UPDATE_FIELDS` (manual linking stays possible, mirroring the other two documents); **joins the claim snapshot/restore field surface** (`loadClaimSnapshot`/`restoreSnapshot` in `commands/claims.ts`) so every undoable claim command round-trips it — without this, any undo would silently drop the link (round-3 jury F-04); **`validateClaimReferences` (`claims.ts:1104-1129`) gains a `creditMemoId → sales_credit_memos` scoped check** so a manually linked memo must exist in-tenant/org (jury F-04 r2), with the established absent-module degradation; exposed in list/detail API responses and as an edit-page form field (`warranty_claims.form.creditMemoId`), mirroring the `salesReturnId` wiring.

**Command** `warranty_claims.claim.create_credit_memo` (new, in `commands/claims.ts`, `isUndoable: true`):

- **Input** (command) `{ organizationId, tenantId, id: uuid, updatedAt?: token }` `.strict()`.
- **Guards:** scoped claim + optimistic lock; `orderId` present (400 `creditMemoRequiresOrder`); status ∈ `CREDIT_MEMO_ELIGIBLE_CLAIM_STATUSES = received | inspecting | resolved`; `creditMemoId` empty (400 `creditMemoAlreadyLinked`); sales present via `resolveOptionalEntityId('sales', 'sales_credit_memo')` (400 `creditMemoSalesUnavailable`).
- **Line eligibility — receipt-proven (reconciles jury F-01/F-24):** non-deleted ∧ `disposition ∈ credit | refund` ∧ `lineStatus ∈ received | inspected | resolved` (NOT `approved` — credit follows the goods) ∧ `orderLineId` present ∧ **credited qty = min(qtyApproved ?? qtyClaimed, qtyReceived ?? 0) > 0** — a line whose units were never received produces no credit; partial receipt credits the received portion. The resolved source order line must also belong to `claim.orderId` (shared ownership check) **and carry the order's own `currency_code`** — a line whose currency differs from the order header currency is skipped into `skippedLineIds`, keeping the memo single-currency (header totals sum line amounts; mixing currencies would produce a nonsense document — round-3 jury F-05/F-09). Fractional credited quantities are eligible (`creditMemoCreateSchema` line quantity is `decimal({ min: 0 })`; credit is arithmetic, not physical units). Ineligible/zero lines → `skippedLineIds`; empty set → 400 `creditMemoNoEligibleLines`. Credit-before-receipt (goodwill / D365 `Credit only`) is a documented roadmap item, not a v1 behavior.
- **Amount contract (exact, discount-aware, percentage-correct — reconciles jury F-02/F-03/F-07/F-14/F-15/F-17/F-22 + round-2 F-05/F-06):** all arithmetic in bigint units at `numeric(18,4)` scale (`CLAIMED_QUANTITY_SCALE` precedent); **no intermediate value is ever rounded — each prorated amount is one fused multiply-then-divide** (`round(total × creditedQtyUnits ÷ orderQtyUnits)`) with **half-up rounding applied exactly once, at the final division**; header totals are exact sums of line values (no re-rounding). For each eligible line, read the order line's `quantity`, `total_net_amount`, `total_gross_amount`, `tax_rate` (percentage points 0–100). Order-line `quantity = 0` → line skipped, no division:
  0. **Source normalization (round-3 jury F-06/F-13 — legacy zero-net lines):** if the order line has `total_gross_amount > 0` but `total_net_amount ≤ 0` (the legacy data class `deriveLineNetFromGross`'s docblock documents, issues #3521/#3036), the net basis for proration is first re-derived exactly as sales would: `netBasis = round(total_gross_amount ÷ (1 + tax_rate/100))`; otherwise `netBasis = total_net_amount`. This restores the `net > 0 ⟺ gross > 0` invariant at the source, so bridge-computed values always persist verbatim.
  1. `proratedGross = round(total_gross_amount × creditedQtyUnits ÷ orderQtyUnits)`, `proratedNet = round(netBasis × creditedQtyUnits ÷ orderQtyUnits)` — one fused multiply-divide each (the quantity units cancel, so the result carries the amount's 4-dp scale — no separate rescaling; round-3 jury F-07); prorating the **discounted line totals** captures what the customer actually paid at line level (naive `unit_price_gross × qty` would over-credit discounted lines).
  2. `baseGross = claimLine.creditAmount ?? proratedGross` — the staff override is a gross-context amount, like every claim-line money field (`nullableDecimal()` = min 0, `data/validators.ts:32/163-166` — negative overrides are impossible by validator, jury F-16).
  3. `lineGross = max(0, baseGross − (restockingFee ?? 0) + (coreCreditAmount ?? 0))` — null money fields count as zero (round-3 jury F-11); **the same clamp shape as the module's approved-rollup** (`max(0, credit − fee + core)`, `lib/stateMachine.ts:110`), with one deliberate difference: the rollup's base is the `creditAmount` override alone, while the memo substitutes `proratedGross` when no override is set (the rollup has no order-line prices to prorate from — round-2 jury F-16); `restockingFee` and `coreCreditAmount` are gross-context amounts (jury F-15/F-17).
  4. `lineNet`: when no override/fee/core adjustment applied (`lineGross === proratedGross`), `lineNet = proratedNet` (pure proration path — no tax formula needed); otherwise `lineNet = round(lineGross ÷ (1 + tax_rate/100))` — **the divisor converts percentage points** (`tax_rate` is 0–100, verified via `deriveLineNetFromGross`, `sales/commands/shared.ts:91`; jury F-07 r1). `lineTax = lineGross − lineNet` (exact subtraction, both paths). **Consistency clamp (r4 F-16/F-28):** if independent rounding ever yields `lineGross > 0 ∧ lineNet ≤ 0` (sub-cent prorations), `lineNet` is re-derived from `lineGross` via the percentage formula — mirroring exactly what sales' `deriveLineNetFromGross` pass-through (`documents.ts:9037-9039`) would do — so persisted values ALWAYS equal bridge values, with no corner exceptions (round-2 jury F-18).
  - **Known v1 limitation (round-2 jury F-07, explicit):** credited amounts derive from LINE-level paid amounts (which include line discounts); **order-level adjustments/discounts are NOT prorated into the credit** — they live on the order document, not its lines, and allocating them is a product decision (which adjustment kinds count, how shipping interacts). Documented in the openApi description; staff can edit the memo; roadmap item below.
  - **Single-document semantics (r4 F-04, explicit):** like `salesReturnId` and `replacementOrderId`, `creditMemoId` is a single link — the bridge executes once per claim. A partial-receipt memo consumes that execution; later arrivals are credited by editing the memo in sales or hand-creating a second one there (the bridge recomputes from scratch, so unlink-and-re-run would double-credit — the guard exists precisely to prevent that). Incremental credit (a per-line credited-qty ledger) is a roadmap item.
  - **Boundary precision (r4 F-03/F-10):** amounts cross the command boundary as zod-coerced JS numbers before sales re-serializes via `toNumericString`. A 4-dp decimal round-trips `string → double → toFixed(4)` exactly for any magnitude below 10^11 units — far beyond realistic memo totals; the memo unit suite asserts the dispatched values survive the round-trip unchanged.
  5. **Memo line unit prices are derived, not copied (jury F-03/F-33):** `unitPriceGross = round(lineGross × QTY_SCALE ÷ creditedQtyUnits)`, `unitPriceNet = round(lineNet × QTY_SCALE ÷ creditedQtyUnits)` — the numerator is rescaled because dividing two same-scale operands would strip the 4-dp scale (dimensional completeness, round-3 jury F-07); the persisted line satisfies `unitPrice × quantity ≈ total` within rounding; totals are authoritative (catalog prices remain visible on the order itself).
  6. Memo line: `{ orderLineId, quantity: creditedQty, currencyCode: orderLine.currency_code, unitPriceNet, unitPriceGross, taxRate: orderLine.tax_rate, taxAmount: lineTax, totalNetAmount: lineNet, totalGrossAmount: lineGross, metadata: { warrantyClaimLineId } }` (per-line traceability — round-3 jury F-12; line metadata persisted, `documents.ts:9041`) plus `name: orderLine.name` only when non-null (null-omission rule — round-2 jury F-11). ## Edge Cases & Failure Scenarios

- **Claim with mixed dispositions** (2× `replace`, 1× `credit`, 1× `restock`): replacement bridge takes exactly the `replace` lines, credit bridge exactly the `credit`/`refund` lines, `skippedLineIds` reports the rest in both — the two documents coexist on one claim, matching D365 `Replace and credit`.
- **Disposition unset** (receiving skipped, straight approve): both bridges 400 `*NoEligibleLines` — dispositions are the semantic trigger; the sales-return bridge (line-status-driven) remains available. Error copy names the missing disposition so staff know to disposition first.
- **Resolved claim, nothing received** (`qtyReceived` null/0 on every line): credit bridge 400 `creditMemoNoEligibleLines` — receipt evidence is per-line, not per-status (jury F-01).
- **Partial receipt** (approved 3, received 2): credited qty = 2; the flash shows the total so staff see the partial credit immediately.
- **Order line deleted/missing in sales / identity-less order line:** per-line skip → `skippedLineIds`; claim-level order row missing → 400 `*RequiresOrder` (stale link surfaces loudly).
- **`creditAmount` set to `'0.0000'`:** an explicit zero override → `lineGross = max(0, 0 − fee + core)`; if every line nets 0 the memo is still created (a zero-total memo is a legitimate audit artifact) — the flash shows the total.
- **Restocking fee exceeds credit:** floored at 0 per line (module rollup convention, `stateMachine.ts:108-110`) — never negative (memo line amounts are `min: 0` in sales validators).
- **Order-line `quantity = 0`** (data anomaly): proration impossible → line skipped, reported.
- **Concurrent double-click:** optimistic-lock header + `*AlreadyLinked` re-check under `PESSIMISTIC_WRITE`; the loser compensates (deletes its just-created document) and 400s — no orphans, no double documents.
- **Compensation delete fails:** 500-class orphan error (never the underlying 400 — single failure boundary above); timeline entry + log carry the orphaned document id; the document carries the claim number. Operator retries with evidence visible — accepted residual (round-3 r2 precedent).
- **Sales module absent (standalone app):** `resolveOptionalEntityId` short-circuits → 400 `*SalesUnavailable` (per-bridge error copy, jury F-13); detail-page buttons additionally hide when the operator lacks the sales features (an app without sales never grants them).
- **Undo after the sales document was edited in sales:** version-token mismatch → 409 abort; the claim stamp is NOT cleared (state stays honest; staff unlink manually if intended).
- **Undo after the sales document was deleted in sales:** scoped lookup finds no row → the delete dispatch is skipped and the claim snapshot restores normally (info log) — undo is never wedged by an already-gone document.
- **Claim re-linked to a different order after lines were added:** each source order line's `order_id` is checked against `claim.orderId`; mismatched lines are skipped into `skippedLineIds` — no mixed-order documents.

## Risks & Impact Review

- **Blast radius: low-medium.** One nullable column; two new commands + routes + detail-page actions; one field added to an existing update field-set, reference validation, and list/detail responses; nine hygiene edits (five test/docs-only). Two deliberate behavior changes to existing paths, both narrow: the sales credit-memo order-link fix (test-anticipated; null → real link) and the round-3 bridge's double-failure error precedence (hygiene item 9).
- **Money correctness (Phase 2) — the round's highest risk:** sales stores the bridge's amounts verbatim, so the arithmetic is load-bearing. Mitigations: the amount contract above is fully deterministic (scaled-integer, half-up, percentage-correct tax, derived unit prices, rollup-mirroring formula); a dedicated unit suite pins every branch (proration, discount, override, fee floor, core credit, fractional qty, zero-qty skip, percentage tax at 23%, rounding at boundaries); the memo total is surfaced in the success flash + timeline payload for immediate human inspection; staff retain full edit rights on the memo in sales.
- **Receipt-proven credit** relies on the receiving workbench recording `qtyReceived` — claims resolved without receiving produce no credit via the bridge (staff can still hand-create memos in sales); this is the intended conservative default, documented in the UI help/error copy.
- **Cross-module writes:** only via `sales.orders.{create,delete}` / `sales.credit_memos.{create,delete}` dispatch — sales' own validation and side effects run; no warranty code touches sales tables directly; RBAC dual-gated at the route (peer feature verbatim).
