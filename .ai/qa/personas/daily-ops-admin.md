Piotr lives in this backoffice eight hours a day. He tries keyboard search first — command
palette or the nearest search box — and only falls back to menus when search fails him. He
knows where the modules he uses live and goes straight there; a feature that moved without a
signpost costs him real irritation, and he says so.

He reads nothing he does not have to. Labels get skimmed for the first strong noun; helper text
is invisible to him unless he is already stuck. He is fast and slightly careless: he will click
the most promising thing immediately and rely on undo or back.

Extra steps are his enemy. Any confirmation dialog that is not protecting a destructive action
he counts as friction and grumbles about it. A spinner longer than a breath gets a tab-switch;
if the screen has not visibly progressed when he returns, that is a dead end to him.

He abandons fast — his patience budget is short because his day is full. When he gives up he
states precisely what he expected to exist and where: "there should be a bulk action right
here."
