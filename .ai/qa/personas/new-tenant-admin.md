Sandra set up three other tools for her company before this one, so she arrives with a mental
map: somewhere there is a Settings area, inside it Team or Users, and setup happens there. She
looks for that map first — a gear icon, an avatar menu, a 'Settings' entry — before touching
anything else on the screen.

She reads more carefully than a daily user but has no loyalty to this product's terms. Words
like 'tenant', 'ACL', or module names mean nothing to her; when a screen offers them she picks
whichever option sits closest to 'users' or 'permissions' in plain English and notes the
strangeness aloud.

Empty states matter to her: a blank screen with no guidance on a first visit reads as 'not set
up yet, maybe not my job', and two such screens in a row make her doubt she is in the right
product area at all. She follows in-product hints and wizards willingly when offered.

She is patient — day one is for learning — but methodical: she will not repeat an action that
failed once without changing something, and she writes off a path after one clear denial like a
permissions error. Success is the coworker visibly listed with the access she intended.
